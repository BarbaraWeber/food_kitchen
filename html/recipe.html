Brazilian Style Rice

This is the recipe I use since I was younger, when I learned how to cook with my family.

Ingredients

  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup of rice. I use basmati rice, but you can use the rice of your preference
  • 2 tablespoons of oil. I use olive oil but you can use any kind of oil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 cups of water

Instructions

  1. I rinse the rice in a strainer until the water comes out clear, then I let it sit in the counter until I finish to prepare the rest of the ingredients.
  2. Place the 4 cups of water in a different pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Prepare the garlic. I start by peeling the garlic gloves and then I place it in my pestle and mortar with a pinch of salt and then I smash it until it becomes a paste.
  4. Now I add the olive oil to a pan and I let it heat up for a little bit. FYI, I don't let it get too hot, because it can burn your garlic. Then I add the garlic paste and let it fry for a few seconds, before adding the rice.
  5. Add the rice following with the 1 teaspoons of salt to the pan the garlic is on and fry it for 1-2 minutes stirring at the same time.
  6. Finally I add the boiling water to the pan until it covers the rice, about two fingers above the rice. Set aside the remaining boiling water because you might need it if the rice is not evenly cooked.
  7. Once the water in the rice starts to boil, I put the lid half way to the pan while it is on medium heat. Once the water starts to lower down I cover the pan completely and lower a little be the heat and let it cook throughly.
  8. I check the rice often to make sure it is not burning. If you see the water complety evaporated and the rice is still not fully cooked, I go ahead and add more water to it, but not a lot just untill it reaches the top of the rice, I grab the rice spoon and kind great a hole on the sides and on the middle so the water spreads evenly through it.
  9. Once it is fully cooked, I turn off the heat and let it rest for a few miutes before I serve it.
